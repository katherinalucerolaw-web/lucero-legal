How to Obtain Permanent Residency in Argentina

A comprehensive guide covering eligibility requirements, application process, timeline expectations, and required documents for obtaining permanent residency.

Updated January 2026 • 10 min read

What is Permanent Residency?

Permanent residency (Residencia Permanente) in Argentina grants foreign nationals the right to live and work in the country indefinitely. Unlike temporary residency, which typically requires annual renewals, permanent residents do not need to renew their status and enjoy most of the same rights as Argentine citizens—except voting.

Permanent residency also serves as a prerequisite for citizenship. After maintaining permanent residency for two years, you become eligible to apply for Argentine citizenship through naturalization.

Who is Eligible?

You may be eligible for permanent residency if you fall into one of these categories:

  • Temporary residents who have maintained legal status for the required period (typically 1-3 years depending on visa category)
  • Spouses of Argentine citizens or permanent residents
  • Parents of Argentine children (children born in Argentina are citizens by birth)
  • MERCOSUR nationals who have completed the temporary residency period
  • Refugees granted protection in Argentina

The Application Process

Step 1: Verify Your Eligibility

Before applying, confirm you meet the requirements for your residency category. Different visa types have different waiting periods before you can convert to permanent status. For example:

  • Rentista visas: typically 3 years of temporary residency
  • MERCOSUR nationals: 2 years of temporary residency
  • Spouses of citizens: immediate eligibility

Step 2: Gather Required Documents

The standard documentation package includes:

  • Valid passport and copies
  • Current temporary residency documentation (Precaria or DNI extranjero)
  • Police background check (Certificado de Antecedentes Penales) from Argentina
  • Proof of address (utility bill or rental contract)
  • Proof of ongoing compliance with original visa requirements

Additional documents may be required depending on your specific situation.

Step 3: Submit Your Application

Applications are submitted to the Dirección Nacional de Migraciones (DNM). You can initiate the process online through the RADEX system (Sistema de Radicación a Distancia de Extranjeros) and then complete an in-person appointment.

Step 4: Attend Your Appointment

During your appointment, officials will verify your identity, review your documentation, and take biometric data. Be prepared to explain your situation and answer questions about your time in Argentina.

Step 5: Await Decision

Processing times vary but typically range from 2-6 months. During this period, you'll receive a "Precaria" (temporary authorization) that allows you to remain legally in the country.

Timeline Expectations

Stage Estimated Time
Document preparation 2-4 weeks
Appointment scheduling 1-4 weeks
Government processing 2-6 months
DNI issuance (after approval) 2-4 weeks

Note: Timelines can vary significantly based on current government processing capacity and the completeness of your application.

Common Challenges

  • Documentation gaps: Missing or incorrectly apostilled documents can delay your application months.
  • Expired background checks: Many documents have validity periods (typically 3-6 months). Timing is crucial.
  • Address verification issues: Informal rental arrangements can complicate proof of residence.
  • Travel during processing: Extended absences from Argentina may affect your application.

Benefits of Permanent Residency

  • No renewal requirements—your status is indefinite
  • Full right to work in any profession
  • Access to public healthcare and education
  • Ability to sponsor family members for residency
  • Path to citizenship after 2 years
  • Easier travel in the region and simplified entry upon return

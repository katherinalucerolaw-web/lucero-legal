Starting a Business in Argentina

Argentina offers significant opportunities for foreign entrepreneurs and investors. However, the legal and regulatory landscape differs substantially from other jurisdictions. Choosing the right corporate structure, understanding tax implications, and ensuring compliance from the start are critical to success.

We guide international clients through every stage of business formation and operation, from initial structuring decisions to ongoing compliance requirements.

Business Structures

SAS (Sociedad por Acciones Simplificada)

Most Popular

The Simplified Joint Stock Company is the most flexible and modern corporate form in Argentina. Introduced in 2017, it offers streamlined formation, minimal capital requirements, and digital registration.

Min. Partners: 1 (can be 100% foreign-owned)
Capital: 2x minimum wage (~$400 USD)
Formation Time: 24-72 hours
Best For: Startups, SMEs, investors

SA (Sociedad An√≥nima)

The traditional Corporation, suitable for larger enterprises or those requiring a more formal governance structure. Required for certain regulated industries.

Min. Partners: 2
Capital: ~$100,000 ARS
Formation Time: 2-4 weeks
Best For: Large enterprises, regulated industries

SRL (Sociedad de Responsabilidad Limitada)

Limited Liability Company. A middle-ground option with characteristics of both partnerships and corporations.

Min. Partners: 2-50
Capital: Variable
Formation Time: 2-3 weeks
Best For: Family businesses, partnerships

Branch Office (Sucursal)

Extension of a foreign parent company. The parent remains liable for branch operations, but it allows operation without forming a new legal entity.

Requirements: Parent company documents, local representative
Best For: Multinationals, temporary projects

Banking & Financial Services

Navigating the Argentine banking system presents unique challenges for foreign-owned businesses. Exchange controls, Central Bank regulations, and compliance requirements require careful attention.

Corporate Bank Accounts

We assist with opening peso and dollar accounts at major Argentine banks, preparing all required documentation and coordinating with bank compliance teams.

International Transfers

Structuring compliant capital inflows using official channels (MULC) or market mechanisms (CCL/MEP) to ensure you receive fair value while meeting all regulatory requirements.

Fintech Compliance

Regulatory guidance for payment processors, crypto-asset service providers (PSAVs), and digital financial services operating under BCRA and CNV oversight.
